  1. 01
    The Tel-Dan Stele: A Roman Boundary Marker of Humiliation on the Via Dolorosa of Mattathias Antigonus II (Summer −37 BCE)
    Din d'Arya · École Celtique · 2026 · Main preprint · EN
    This study argues that the Tel Dan Stele was erected in summer 37 BCE as a Roman-Herodian boundary marker of humiliation placed on the Via Dolorosa of the last Hasmonean king Mattathias Antigonus II. Evidence rests on morphology, geography, psalmic semantics, historical contextualisation, and the critique of conventional ninth-century BCE dating.

  9. 09
    Tel Dan Stele: A Historical and Archaeological Impossibility of a 9th-Century BCE Dating
    Din d'Arya · École Celtique · 2026 · Preprint · EN
    Two independent sets of anomalies undermine the ninth-century BCE dating of the Tel Dan Stele: a historical anomaly (the forty-year Hazael Glitch) and an archaeological anomaly (secondary context, unpublished final report, Stratum IVA re-dated by one century by Arie, Tel Aviv 35, 2008). The three pillars of the consensus — palaeographic, historical, and archaeological — do not converge independently.
    Stèle de Tel Dan Hazael Glitch stratigraphie Iron Age II Arie 2008 EN
