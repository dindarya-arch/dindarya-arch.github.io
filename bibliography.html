The Tel Dan Stele's Mention of the Psalms of Solomon (63 to 44 BCE)
Din d'Arya · École Celtique · 2025 · Preprint · EN / FR
Philological and thematic analysis establishing that the Psalms of Solomon predate the Tel Dan Stele, through 17 thematic parallels and 6 structural silences. Uses a strictly relative chronological argumentation without direct attribution, treating both texts as products of the first-century BCE Herodian bilingual milieu.

04
The Tel-Dan Stele: A Roman Boundary Marker of Humiliation on the Via Dolorosa of Mattathias Antigonus II (Summer −37 BCE)
Din d'Arya · École Celtique · 2025 · Preprint · EN
Main preprint. Argues that the Tel Dan Stele was erected in summer 37 BCE as a Roman boundary marker of humiliation, identifying the "House of David" inscription as a politically motivated fabrication placed on the Via Dolorosa of the last Hasmonean king Mattathias Antigonus II. Evidence rests on SVO word-order anomalies incompatible with authentic 9th-century Aramaic, with the Mesha Stele as a control corpus.
